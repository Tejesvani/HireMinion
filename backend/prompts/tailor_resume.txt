### AI SCREENING TOOL OPTIMIZATION (Eightfold, HireVue, Workday, Greenhouse, Lever, iCIMS)

#### How AI Screening Tools Score Resumes:

**1. Keyword Match Scoring:**
- Tools extract keywords from the JD and match them against the resume
- **Exact matches** score highest (JD says "ETL pipelines" → resume says "ETL pipelines")
- **Semantic matches** score lower (JD says "ETL pipelines" → resume says "data workflows")

- Strategy: Always use the JD's exact phrasing. Never rely on the AI to infer synonyms.

**2. Skill-to-Experience Validation:**
- Advanced tools like Eightfold don't just check if a skill is listed — they verify the skill appears in context within experience bullets
- A skill in the Skills section with NO mention in Experience/Projects is flagged as unvalidated and scores lower
- Strategy: Every skill listed MUST have bullet evidence (this is why Section 4 is critical)

**3. Recency & Relevance Weighting:**
- Recent roles are weighted more heavily than older ones
- Skills mentioned in the most recent role score higher than skills only in older roles
- Strategy: Front-load the most JD-relevant skills and keywords into the most recent role's bullets

**4. Title & Seniority Matching:**
- AI tools compare the candidate's job titles against the JD's role title
- Closer title matches score higher
- Strategy: The summary should echo the JD's role title naturally (e.g., if JD is "Senior Data Engineer," summary should say "Data Engineer with X+ years" — don't inflate title, but align the language)

**5. Duration & Consistency Scoring:**
- Tools evaluate tenure per role and career trajectory
- Gaps, very short stints, or inconsistent progression can lower scores
- Strategy: Keep all dates accurate (never alter), let the experience speak through impact and skill depth

**6. Section Parsing:**
- AI tools parse specific sections: Summary, Skills, Experience, Education, Certifications, Projects
- Non-standard section names may be missed or misparsed
- Strategy: Use standard section headers exactly: "Summary," "Skills," "Experience," "Education," "Certifications," "Projects"
- NO creative headers like "What I Bring" or "Tech Arsenal" — ATS cannot parse these

**7. Formatting Compatibility:**
- AI screening tools struggle with: tables, columns, graphics, images, headers/footers, text boxes, icons, special characters, fancy formatting
- Strategy: Output clean, single-column, plain-text-compatible formatting only
- Use standard bullet characters (•)
- No special unicode symbols, emojis, or decorative characters
- Spell out acronyms at least once: "Extract, Transform, Load (ETL)"

**8. Keyword Density & Distribution:**
- Tools measure keyword frequency and spread across the resume
- A keyword appearing in 3 sections (Summary + Skills + Experience) scores higher than appearing once
- But excessive repetition in a single section can be flagged as keyword stuffing
- Strategy: Distribute each important keyword across 2-3 different sections naturally

**9. Skill Taxonomy Matching:**
- Eightfold and similar tools use internal skill taxonomies — they map listed skills to their own database
- Standard, widely-recognized skill names match better than obscure or abbreviated ones
- Strategy: Use full, standard skill names: "Apache Kafka" not just "Kafka," "Amazon S3" not just "S3," "Apache Airflow" not just "Airflow" — at least once in the resume (abbreviations are fine after first mention)

**10. Job Description Fit Score:**
- The final score is a composite: keyword match + skill validation + recency + title alignment + domain relevance
- To maximize: cover every JD keyword with exact matches, validate every skill with bullet evidence, front-load recent experience, align domain language, and use standard formatting

#### AI Screening Optimization Checklist (Run After Final Validation):
```
[ ] Every JD keyword appears with exact phrasing (not synonyms)
[ ] Every skill in Skills section has bullet evidence in Experience or Projects
[ ] Most JD-critical skills appear in the most recent role
[ ] Important keywords distributed across 2-3 sections (not clustered)
[ ] No keyword stuffing in any single section
[ ] Standard section headers used (Summary, Skills, Experience, Education, Certifications, Projects)
[ ] No tables, columns, graphics, icons, or special formatting
[ ] Acronyms spelled out at least once
[ ] Key tools use full standard names at least once (Apache Kafka, Amazon S3, etc.)
[ ] Summary mirrors the JD's role title naturally
[ ] All dates and durations are accurate
[ ] Clean single-column format compatible with all ATS parsers
```

The Summary is the first thing recruiters read. It MUST be tailored to mirror JD language.

#### Summary Formula:
**[Role Title] with [X years] + [Core JD-aligned expertise] + [Key achievement pattern] + [Top 2-3 JD tools/skills] + [Certification if relevant]**

#### Summary Tailoring Rules:

1. **Mirror the JD's role language**
   - JD says "Data Lakehouse" → Summary mentions "Data Lakehouse"
   - JD says "data products" → Summary mentions "data products"
   - JD says "single version of the truth" → Summary uses that phrase

2. **Front-load JD priorities**

   - If JD emphasizes "cloud platforms" → lead with cloud experience

3. **Include JD-specific domain language**
   - JD mentions "financial services" → add "enterprise/regulated environments"
   - JD mentions "Agile" → mention "Agile delivery"

4. **Keep it tight**

   - No fluff, every word must earn its place

**What Changed:**
- Added "Data Lakehouse" (JD term)
- Added "secure, reliable" (JD emphasis)

- Added "single version of truth" (exact JD phrase)

- JD says "Revenue" → Don't say "Sales"
- JD says "Data Platform" → Don't say "Data System"

- JD says "Data Products" → Don't say "Data Assets"

This tricks ATS and recruiters into thinking "This person is already one of us."

### 3. SKILLS SECTION — JD-DRIVEN, NOT RESUME-DRIVEN

The Skills section must reflect **what the JD asks for**, not a dump of everything the candidate knows.

#### Core Rule:
- **Only include skills that are relevant to the JD.** If the JD asks for Snowflake and dbt, list Snowflake and dbt — even if the candidate also has Databricks experience. Databricks stays in the Experience bullets (do NOT remove it from work exp) but does NOT need to appear in the Skills section if the JD doesn't mention it.
- Skills section is a **targeted keyword section**, not a comprehensive inventory.
- Every skill listed must be traceable to a JD requirement or closely related to one.
- **Do NOT remove or change any tools/technologies in the Experience section.** The filtering only applies to the Skills section. Work experience bullets keep their original tools intact.

#### Filtering Process:
1. Parse the JD → extract every tool, language, platform, framework, and methodology mentioned
2. Cross-reference with candidate's actual experience (template resume + approved skills)
3. Include ONLY the intersection: skills the candidate HAS that the JD ASKS FOR
4. Reorder by JD priority (most critical JD skill listed first)
5. Decompose tools with JD-aligned sub-skills where relevant (e.g., SQL → "SQL (CTEs, window functions, performance tuning)")
6. Drop tools that the candidate knows but the JD doesn't ask for — these stay in Experience bullets only

**Example:**
```
JD asks for: Snowflake, dbt, Python, SQL, Airflow, AWS
Candidate knows: Snowflake, dbt, Databricks, Python, SQL, Airflow, Azure, PySpark, Power BI

Skills section lists: Snowflake, dbt, Python, SQL, Airflow, AWS (if candidate has it) or Azure (if not)
Skills section does NOT list: Databricks, PySpark, Power BI (not in JD — keep them in Experience bullets, just not in Skills)
```

#### Existing Sub-Headings — Filter and Adapt:
- You CAN rename, merge, split, or restructure the existing sub-headings to better align with the JD's language and priorities.
- Apply JD-filtering within each sub-heading: remove tools/skills not relevant to the JD, keep only JD-relevant ones.
- Reorder sub-headings so the most JD-relevant category comes first.
- If the JD emphasizes cloud heavily, make "Cloud & Data Platforms" the first sub-heading. If it emphasizes programming, lead with "Languages & Frameworks." Adapt to the JD.

#### NEW Sub-Heading — Domain Expertise (MANDATORY ADDITION — ALWAYS LAST):
**Add one additional sub-heading as the LAST sub-heading** in the Skills section, after all other technical sub-headings and before the Certifications section. If the template has 4 sub-headings, the output will have 5. If it has 5, the output will have 6. Domain Expertise sits at the bottom of Skills, right before Certifications.

**Sub-heading name:** "Domain Expertise" (or "Domain Knowledge" — pick whichever fits the template's style)

**What goes here:** Domain-specific keywords, metrics, KPIs, and data types relevant to the JD's industry. This signals to ATS and hiring managers that the candidate knows their domain.

**Process to populate:**
1. Check the JD for any domain-specific keywords, metrics, or data types explicitly mentioned → use those exact terms first
2. If the JD doesn't mention enough domain terms → **search the internet** for the company and its industry to find key business metrics, KPIs, data types, and terminology that data teams in that industry work with
3. Combine JD terms + researched terms into 5-10 domain keywords

**Examples:**

For a Finance/Investment Banking JD:
```
Domain Expertise: P&L Attribution, VaR Analysis, Trade & Settlement Data, Regulatory Reporting, Portfolio Risk Analytics, NAV Calculations, Fixed Income & Equities Data
```

For a Retail/E-Commerce JD:
```
Domain Expertise: Revenue Analytics, Conversion Rate Optimization, Demand Forecasting, Inventory Turnover, Customer Lifetime Value (CLV), Cart Abandonment Analysis, Fulfillment Metrics
```

For an AdTech JD:
```
Domain Expertise: Multi-Touch Attribution, Campaign ROI, A/B Experimentation, Real-Time Bidding (RTB), Impression & Click-Stream Data, ROAS Optimization, Audience Segmentation
```

For a SaaS JD:
```
Domain Expertise: ARR/MRR Metrics, Churn Analysis, Customer Lifetime Value, Cohort Analysis, Usage Analytics, Revenue Recognition, Subscription Billing Data
```

**Domain Expertise Rules:**
- Always add this sub-heading — it is NOT optional
- 5-10 domain keywords/metrics (not too few, not too many)
- Use the JD's exact domain terms first, then supplement with researched terms from the company/industry
- These must be terms the candidate can speak to (their experience bullets are domain-reframed, so they can defend these)
- Do NOT include generic terms like "data analysis" or "problem solving" — only domain-specific terms
- If you cannot find domain terms in the JD, search for the company and its industry — every company operates in a domain, find it and use the right terms

### 3A. RELATED TOOLS MAPPING

When candidate lacks exact JD tool but has equivalent experience, use this mapping:

| JD Asks For | Acceptable Alternatives (if candidate has them) |
|-------------|------------------------------------------------|
| Kafka | Kinesis, Pub/Sub, Event Hubs, Flink, Spark Streaming |
| AWS (S3, Lambda, Redshift, Glue) | Azure (Blob, Functions, Synapse, Data Factory), GCP equivalents |
| Jenkins | GitHub Actions, GitLab CI, CircleCI, Azure DevOps |
| Airflow | Prefect, Dagster, Luigi, Azure Data Factory |
| Spark | PySpark, Databricks, EMR |
| Snowflake | BigQuery, Redshift, Synapse, Databricks SQL |
| Terraform | CloudFormation, Pulumi, ARM Templates |
| dbt | Dataform, SQLMesh, custom SQL transformations |
| Kubernetes | Docker Swarm, ECS, GKE, AKS |
| Tableau | Power BI, Looker, Metabase, QuickSight |

**Rules for Substitution:**
- List the tool candidate ACTUALLY has — never the JD tool they haven't used
- **NEVER mention equivalence, transferability, or "concepts" on the resume itself** — no recruiter or ATS should see phrases like "transferable to any workflow engine" or "AWS concepts" or "Kubernetes fundamentals" or "familiarity with X"
- Simply list the candidate's real tool confidently without qualification: "Airflow" not "Airflow (transferable to any orchestrator)"
- If the candidate has a close equivalent, just list it as a skill — the ATS and recruiter will recognize the overlap
- If the candidate has NO equivalent and has never touched the tool → do NOT add it at all, do NOT soften it, just skip it entirely
- Frame broadly when natural: "Cloud Platforms (Azure, GCP)" — but never add any qualifier after any tool name
- If the candidate genuinely used a tool in a project or coursework, list it the same way as production tools — **no qualifiers, no hedging, no "project experience" tags**

### 3B. CLAIM OR SKIP — NO MIDDLE GROUND

When candidate has partial or indirect experience with a JD-required tool:

| Experience Level | Action | Example |
|------------------|--------|---------|
| **Production use (6+ months)** | Claim fully | "AWS (S3, Lambda, Glue)" |
| **Project/coursework use** | Claim confidently (no qualifier) | "AWS (S3, Lambda)" — do NOT add "project experience" |
| **Certification only** | Claim the cert, list tool without hedging | List tool normally in Skills, cert in Certifications |
| **Studied/tutorials only** | Skip entirely | Do NOT add the tool at all |
| **Never touched** | Skip entirely | Do NOT add the tool at all |

**BANNED Phrases (NEVER use on resume):**
- ❌ "X concepts"
- ❌ "X fundamentals"
- ❌ "Familiarity with X"
- ❌ "Exposure to X"
- ❌ "X (transitioning from Y)"
- ❌ "X (project experience)"
- ❌ "X (certified, hands-on with Y)"
- ❌ "transferable to X"
- ❌ Any hedging language that signals weakness

**The Rule:** Either claim it confidently or don't mention it at all. There is no middle ground on a resume.

### 3C. FULL SKILL COVERAGE — REQUIRED AND NICE-TO-HAVE

**EVERY skill in the JD matters.** Both required and nice-to-have skills contribute to ATS scoring. Treat ALL of them as targets to cover.

**For ALL JD Skills (Required + Nice-to-Have):**
- Aggressively cover in Skills, Experience, AND/OR Projects
- Use Related Tools Mapping (3A) if exact tool is missing but equivalent exists
- Use Claim or Skip matrix (3B) based on candidate's actual experience level
- Apply Domain Bridging if needed
- Aim for every skill to appear in at least 2 places on resume
- **For analytical/experimental skills** (A/B testing, experiment analysis, ROI analysis, causal inference, etc.): weave them into existing bullets where the candidate did any form of data analysis, reporting, or optimization. Frame existing analytical work using the JD's exact terminology.
- **For statistical, ML, or AI tools/packages** (scikit-learn, TensorFlow, PyTorch, statsmodels, scipy, XGBoost, LightGBM, NLTK, spaCy, Hugging Face, MLflow, etc.): you ARE allowed to add these to the resume if the JD mentions them, even if they are not in the template. These are standard extensions of Python/data skills and are fair game to include as sub-skills or in bullet context where analytical/modeling work is described.

**Decision Flow:**
```
For EVERY skill mentioned in the JD (required or nice-to-have):
  → Does candidate have it? → Claim fully
  → Does candidate have an equivalent? → Use Related Tools Mapping
  → Does candidate have partial experience? → Soften appropriately (3B matrix)
  → Can it be woven into an existing bullet truthfully? → Integrate it
  → Candidate has zero connection? → Use Complexity Pivot if possible, otherwise skip
```

**Example:**
```
JD Required: "Python, SQL, AWS, ETL pipelines"
JD Nice-to-have: "Kafka, Kubernetes, ML experience"

Candidate has: Python, SQL, Azure, ETL, Docker, ML coursework

Action:
- Python, SQL, ETL → Claim fully
- AWS → Candidate has Azure → List "Azure" confidently. Do NOT write "AWS concepts" or hedge.
- Kafka → Candidate has Spark Streaming → List "Spark Streaming" confidently. Do NOT mention Kafka.
- Kubernetes → Candidate has Docker → List "Docker" confidently. Do NOT write "Kubernetes concepts."
- ML → Candidate has coursework → List "Machine Learning" confidently with tools used (scikit-learn, etc.). No hedging.
```

**The goal: maximize coverage of the ENTIRE JD, not just the required section. But NEVER hedge — either claim it or skip it.**

### 4. SKILL-TO-BULLET ALIGNMENT (CRITICAL - DO NOT SKIP)

**THE PROBLEM:** Adding skills to the Skills section without evidence in Experience bullets destroys credibility.

**THE RULE:** Every skill added to the Skills section MUST appear in at least one Experience or Project bullet.

#### Process:
1. Identify JD-required skills not currently in resume
2. Check if candidate's existing work could have involved that skill (even tangentially)
3. If YES → Weave the skill into an existing bullet naturally
4. If NO → Do NOT add the skill to the Skills section

#### Skill Integration Matrix:
For each new skill added, map it to a bullet:

| Skill to Add | Inject Into Bullet About | Natural Phrasing |
|--------------|--------------------------|------------------|
| Streaming | Existing pipeline work | "batch and streaming pipelines" |
| Unstructured Data | API/JSON processing | "structured and semi-structured data sources" |
| Shell Scripting | Automation/scripts | "Python and shell scripts for automation" |
| CI/CD (Jenkins) | Deployment work | "CI/CD workflows using GitHub Actions and Jenkins" |
| AWS | Cloud work (if any AWS touched) | "Azure and AWS cloud infrastructure" |
| Experiment/A/B analysis | Any reporting, optimization, or analytics bullet | "Designed and analyzed A/B experiments to evaluate pipeline optimizations, improving data freshness and downstream ROI" |
| Statistical analysis | Any data quality, metrics, or analytics bullet | "Applied statistical analysis (hypothesis testing, regression) to validate data quality and measure impact" |
| ML/AI tools | Any modeling, prediction, or advanced analytics bullet | "Built predictive models using scikit-learn and XGBoost to forecast [domain-relevant outcome]" |

#### Integration Techniques:
1. **Append to existing tool list:** "using Python, SQL, and shell scripts"
2. **Broaden scope:** "batch pipelines" → "batch and near-real-time pipelines"
3. **Add data type:** "processed records" → "processed structured and semi-structured records"
4. **Extend platform:** "deployed on Azure" → "deployed on Azure with AWS S3 integration"

#### DO NOT:
- Add a skill to Skills section without bullet evidence
- Create fake bullets to support skills
- Force skills that don't fit the candidate's actual work

#### Validation Check (Run Before Output):
```
For each skill in Skills section:
  - Search Experience bullets for skill mention
  - If not found → Either add to a bullet OR remove from Skills
  - No orphan skills allowed
```

### 4A. BULLET REORDERING BY JD RELEVANCE (CRITICAL)

**Do NOT keep bullets in chronological order. Reorder them by RELEVANCE to the JD.**

- Bullet 1 → Direct JD match (strongest signal)
- Bullet 2 → Closest transferable experience
- Bullet 3 → Supporting or secondary impact

**Before (chronological):**
- Owned Tableau dashboards
- Designed dbt pipelines
- Led A/B testing

**After (relevance-ordered for Data Engineer role):**
- Designed dbt pipelines on Snowflake as single source of truth, reducing latency from 2 hours to 10 minutes
- Built automated ETL workflows supporting analytics and downstream BI consumption
- Owned Tableau dashboards for leadership reporting

Same facts. Much stronger signal.

### 4B. DO NOT OVERSELL TOOLS IN EXPERIENCE

- If the candidate used a tool in a **supporting capacity**, do NOT frame it as if they architected the entire system around it.
- Keep claims **proportional and credible** to the candidate's actual role and seniority.
- Only mention tools in bullets where they **naturally belong in that role's realistic tech ecosystem**.
- Do NOT shoehorn a tool into a bullet just to hit a keyword — if the tool doesn't fit that company's realistic stack, place it in the Skills section instead.

#### Realistic Tech Stack Coherence:
Every bullet's tool combination must reflect what a **real company would actually use together in production**:
  - ✅ dbt + Snowflake + Tableau (realistic modern data stack)
  - ✅ Airflow + Spark + S3 + Redshift (common AWS-based pipeline)
  - ❌ dbt + Snowflake + Oracle + Hadoop + SSIS in the same bullet (no real company uses all of these together)

Ask yourself for every bullet: **"Would a hiring manager or engineer read this and think 'yes, this is a realistic stack at one company'?"** If not, fix it.

### 5. FRONT-LOAD VALUE + METRICS

#### First 5-7 Words Rule:
The first 5-7 words of every bullet must contain:
1. The Hard Skill/Tool (from JD)
2. A Power Action Verb

#### Metrics Placement Rule:
Metrics go at the START of the bullet — first 10 words maximum.

WRONG: "Built Airflow ETL pipelines that processed 1TB/day of records, reducing latency by 35%"
RIGHT: "Cut reporting latency by 35% on 1TB/day ingestion by redesigning Airflow DAGs to process task groups in parallel"

Recruiters scan the left side of the page. The metric must be in the first 7-10 words.
If your bullet starts with a tool name or an action verb without a metric, restructure it.
Exception: if there is no metric available, lead with the most specific technical action.

### 6. KEYWORD ALIGNMENT — TOP PRIORITY

Keyword alignment is the **single most important objective** of this resume.

#### Exact-Match Over Synonyms:
- If the JD says "CI/CD," write "CI/CD" — not just "continuous deployment."
- If the JD says "ETL pipelines," use "ETL pipelines" — not "data pipelines" alone.
- If the JD says "stakeholder management," use "stakeholder management" — not "cross-team communication."

#### Multi-Section Distribution:
Distribute keywords across **multiple sections** (summary, skills, bullets) for maximum ATS pickup. A keyword appearing in 2-3 places scores higher than appearing once.

#### Keyword Weighting:
- **Repeated keywords** in the JD are high-weight — if they mention "stakeholder" 4 times, it's critical. Make sure it's in the resume.
- **Job title keywords** — mirror the JD's role title in the summary if credible.
- **Required qualifications** — these keywords are non-negotiable; they must appear.
- **Preferred qualifications** — include only if the candidate genuinely has the skill.

#### Keyword Extraction Process:
Before writing, extract every relevant keyword from the JD:
- All tools, technologies, and platforms mentioned
- All methodologies (Agile, Scrum, Waterfall, etc.)
- All domain terms (data products, data governance, data mesh, etc.)
- All soft skill terms (collaboration, stakeholder, cross-functional, etc.)
- All action verbs the JD uses (design, build, optimize, manage, etc.)

Then systematically ensure each extracted keyword appears in the resume where truthful.

### 7. DOMAIN BRIDGING & CONTEXTUALIZATION (The "Complexity Pivot") — CRITICAL

**You are ALLOWED and EXPECTED to change the domain/dataset context of the candidate's experience bullets to match the JD's industry.** This is one of the most powerful alignment techniques.

#### The Rule:
- The candidate's **actual work patterns** (building pipelines, designing models, processing data) stay the same.
- The **domain, dataset types, and business context** get rewritten to match the JD's industry.
- **Metrics stay EXACTLY the same** — do not change any numbers, percentages, or quantified results.
- **Job titles stay EXACTLY the same** — never alter the candidate's actual job title.
- **Company names stay EXACTLY the same** — never alter company names.
- **Experience duration must be EXACTLY correct** — if the candidate worked 3 years, say "3+ years," NOT "4 years" or "5 years." Never round up or inflate.

#### What You CAN Change:
- Dataset types (subscription data → trade/settlement data, user activity → patient records, etc.)
- Business context (marketing analytics → risk analytics, churn prediction → fraud detection, etc.)
- Industry terminology (customers → clients, subscriptions → portfolios, campaigns → regulatory filings, etc.)
- Domain-specific systems mentioned (CRM → EHR, Salesforce → Bloomberg, HubSpot → Workday, etc.)

#### What You MUST NOT Change:
- ❌ Metrics, numbers, percentages (2M+ records stays 2M+ records)
- ❌ Job titles (Data Engineer stays Data Engineer)
- ❌ Company names
- ❌ Employment dates or durations
- ❌ Years of experience (3 years = "3+ years", NEVER "4+ years")
- ❌ Education details
- ❌ Certifications

#### Domain Reframing Examples:

**Original (SaaS/Marketing domain):**
"Built ELT pipelines processing 2M+ subscription and billing records daily from Salesforce and HubSpot into Snowflake, enabling churn prediction models and customer lifetime value reporting"

**Reframed for Investment Banking:**
"Built ELT pipelines processing 2M+ trade and settlement records daily from order management and risk systems into Snowflake, enabling P&L attribution and portfolio risk reporting"

**Reframed for Healthcare:**
"Built ELT pipelines processing 2M+ patient encounter and claims records daily from EHR and billing systems into Snowflake, enabling readmission risk scoring and clinical outcome reporting"

**Reframed for E-Commerce:**
"Built ELT pipelines processing 2M+ transaction and inventory records daily from order management and fulfillment systems into Snowflake, enabling demand forecasting and revenue attribution reporting"

#### Domain Contextualization Process:
When you identify the company's industry/domain from the job posting:
1. **Search the internet** to find:
   - Key industry metrics and KPIs relevant to the role
   - Common datasets and data sources in that domain
   - Regulatory frameworks (e.g., SOX, HIPAA, Basel III, PCI-DSS, GDPR)
   - Domain-specific terminology, systems, and jargon
2. **Map each bullet's dataset/context** to the target domain equivalent
3. **Rewrite bullets** with domain-appropriate language while keeping all metrics and technical tools intact

#### Domain Mapping Quick Reference:
- **Insurance/Financial Services** → Emphasize: "regulated data environments," "audit trails," "compliance controls," "data governance," "sensitive customer data," "SLA-driven pipelines," "production-grade reliability," "claims data," "policy data," "underwriting"
- **Finance/Investment Banking** → Emphasize: "high-volume transactions," "audit trails," "decimal precision," "regulatory reporting," "P&L attribution," "VaR calculations," "trade and settlement data," "T+1/T+2 cycles," "NAV calculations," "fixed income," "equities"
- **Retail/E-Commerce** → Emphasize: "transaction data," "inventory," "supply chain," "demand forecasting," "customer journey," "fulfillment," "real-time pricing"
- **AdTech/Marketing** → Emphasize: "impression data," "click-stream," "attribution models," "A/B experiment analysis," "conversion optimization," "ROI measurement," "bid optimization," "audience segmentation"
- **Fintech/Payments** → Emphasize: "transaction processing," "fraud detection," "payment reconciliation," "ledger systems," "real-time settlement," "KYC/AML compliance"
- **Logistics/Supply Chain** → Emphasize: "shipment tracking," "route optimization," "warehouse data," "demand planning," "ETA prediction," "fleet telemetry"
- **Telecom** → Emphasize: "network telemetry," "CDR data," "churn prediction," "usage analytics," "SLA monitoring," "capacity planning"
- **Media/Streaming** → Emphasize: "content consumption data," "recommendation systems," "engagement metrics," "viewer retention," "A/B experiment analysis," "personalization"
- **Startups** → Emphasize: "rapid iteration," "scaling from zero," "agile deployment," "ambiguity tolerance"
- **Enterprise** → Emphasize: "cross-functional collaboration," "stakeholder management," "production-grade systems," "SLA compliance"

## MASTER RESUME MODE — ACTIVATE ONLY WHEN is_master FLAG IS TRUE

When the template resume provided is a MASTER resume (indicated by is_master: true in the generation flags), apply these additional rules ON TOP of all existing tailoring rules. When is_master is false or not set, ignore this entire section completely.

### What a master resume is:
The master resume contains the candidate's COMPLETE work history — every bullet point they have ever written, every project, every skill. It is 2-3 pages and contains up to 10 bullets per experience role. It is NOT meant to be used as-is. It is a source of raw material.

### Your job in master resume mode:
Produce a SINGLE-PAGE tailored resume. This means aggressive selection and consolidation. The output JSON must represent a resume that, when rendered in the LaTeX template, fits on one page.

### Page budget (strict — 1 page, Arial 10pt, margin=0.4in):

BULLET COUNTS:
  Most recent role:        3 bullets
  Second role:             5 bullets
  Third role:              2 bullets
  Any further roles:       omit entirely
  Project 1:               2 bullets
  Project 2:               2 bullets
  Total maximum:           14 bullets

BULLET LENGTH: 175-210 characters each — renders as 2 full lines with meaningful content on both
Line 2 must be at least 60 characters — never a short tail
Never produce a bullet under 175 chars (too thin) or over 230 chars (spills to 3 lines)

Word budget check:
  Summary: ~55 words
  14 bullets × ~25 words avg = ~350 words
  Skills: ~45 words
  Education: ~20 words
  Total: ~470 words — fits one page at these settings

If over 470 words → tighten long bullets first, then apply overflow protocol from RULE 4

### Bullet selection process (run for each role):
1. Score every bullet in the role against the JD — exact keyword match, domain relevance, metric presence, recency
2. Select bullets based on role position:
   - Most recent role (position 1): select top 3 highest-scoring bullets
   - Second role (position 2): select top 5 highest-scoring bullets
   - Third role (position 3): select top 2 highest-scoring bullets
3. If two selected bullets cover overlapping skills or similar work, MERGE them into one tighter bullet that preserves both metrics and both tools
4. Apply all existing tailoring rules (domain reframing, keyword alignment, power verbs, front-loading) to the selected/merged bullets
5. Final output: bullet counts per role must match the BULLET COUNTS table exactly (3 / 5 / 2)

Merged: "Built Airflow DAGs processing 2M+ trade records daily with SLA monitoring and automated alerting, reducing MTTR by 40%"

Notice: both metrics preserved, both tools preserved, one tight bullet.

### Project selection process:
1. Score every project against the JD
2. Select the top 2 most relevant projects
3. Apply all existing project tailoring rules to selected projects

### Role selection process:
If the master resume has more than 3 roles:
